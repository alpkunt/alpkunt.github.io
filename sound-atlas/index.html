How to read this. Each tradition is scored 0–10 on nineteen axes — tempo, percussion, plucked strings, bowed strings, wind & reed, brass, vocal centrality, harmony, drone, ornamentation, microtonality, syncopation, improvisation, electronic emphasis, danceability, melancholy, festivity, spiritual/ceremonial use and acoustic rootsiness. Scores are standardized, and affinity between two traditions is a Gaussian function of their distance across all axes (100 = identical sound). Clusters come from k‑means; the map from multidimensional scaling.

A caveat worth hearing. These scores are an informed heuristic, not a survey of every song. National traditions are internally vast — "India" or "China" flatten a dozen distinct regional musics into one point. Read this as a conversation starter about how sounds relate, not a verdict. Numbers are relative and comparative, never absolute.

Method & Data

The score behind the sound.

No web sources, no scraped dataset — just a transparent scoring table and some plain math on top of it. Here's exactly how the atlas was made, and the numbers it runs on.

How it was built
  1. Score — each tradition rated 0–10 on 19 sonic axes (the table below).
  2. Standardize — z‑score every axis so no single one dominates.
  3. Measure — Euclidean distance across all 780 tradition pairs.
  4. Affinity — distance mapped to 0–100 by a Gaussian falloff.
  5. Cluster — k‑means (k = 6) groups the similar sounds.
  6. Flatten — classical MDS drops 19‑D down to the 2‑D sound map.
Sources & honesty

The 0–10 scores are a hand‑built heuristic drawn from general musical knowledge — the instruments, scales, rhythms and textures each tradition is broadly known for. They were not pulled from a database, a survey, or any live web source, so read every number as one informed, comparative opinion rather than a measured fact.

A single national label also flattens huge regional variety into one point — “India” or “China” could each be several rows on their own. Everything downstream, though, is plain, reproducible math on exactly the table below: change a number and the whole atlas re‑draws.
